How to play — basic rules ›

The goal

Be the first to peg 121 points over a series of deals. You score both during the play and when hands are counted (“the show”). The deal passes one seat each hand.

The deal & the crib

Each player is dealt a small hand and throws a card (two, heads-up) into the crib — a bonus hand that belongs to the dealer. The crib always holds four cards and is counted, with the starter, like any other hand. How many cards are dealt and who throws depends on the number of players — each game here sets that up for you.

The cut — the starter

After the throws, the deck is cut to turn up one starter card. It’s shared by every hand and the crib when counting. If the starter is a Jack, the dealer immediately pegs 2 (“his heels”).

The play (pegging)

Players take turns laying a card face-up, calling the running total out loud. The count may never pass 31; if you can’t play without going over you say “Go.” Score as you lay cards down:

  • Make the count exactly 15 → 2 · make exactly 31 → 2
  • Pair the previous card → 2 (three of a kind → 6, four → 12)
  • Complete a run of 3+ with the most recent cards (any order) → 1 per card
  • Go / last card: the last player able to lay a card pegs 1 (when no one else can play, or at the final card of the deal)

The show (counting hands)

When all cards are played, hands are counted in order — starting with the player to the dealer’s left, the dealer last, then the crib. Combine each hand’s four cards with the starter and score (aces = 1, face cards = 10):

  • Fifteens — every combination of cards adding to 15 → 2 each
  • Pairs — 2 each (so three of a kind = 6, four = 12)
  • Runs — three or more in sequence → 1 per card (duplicates count)
  • Flush — four cards one suit → 4; if the starter matches too → 5 (the crib only scores a flush when all five share a suit)
  • Nobs — holding the Jack of the starter’s suit → 1

The best possible hand is 29: three 5s plus the Jack of the cut’s suit, with the fourth 5 turned as the starter.
